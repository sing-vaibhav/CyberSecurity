Common Threats

What attackers use against you

Recognising the pattern is half the battle.

🎣 PhishingCritical

Fake emails or texts pretending to be your bank or a delivery service — designed to steal your password or money.

Never click links in unexpected messages. Go to the website directly.
🔐 Weak PasswordsCritical

Short or reused passwords are cracked in seconds. One fallen account leads attackers to try the same credentials everywhere.

Use a password manager. Every site needs a unique password.
💸 Online ScamsHigh

Fake jobs, romance scams, and lottery wins that take your money and disappear.

If it sounds too good to be true, it is. Research before you pay.
📡 Public Wi-FiHigh

Café and airport networks can be monitored or faked by attackers sitting nearby.

Use mobile data for anything sensitive. Avoid logging in on public Wi-Fi.
🦠 MalwareHigh

Malicious software hidden in downloads or fake apps that spies on you or locks your files for ransom.

Only install apps from official stores. Keep your device updated.
🎭 ImpersonationMedium

Attackers pretend to be Microsoft, your bank, or a family member — creating urgency to make you act fast.

Hang up and call the official number yourself to verify.

Essential Rules

8 habits that protect you

Security is about habits, not tools.

1

Use 2-Factor Authentication

Even if your password leaks, a second step stops attackers from getting in.

2

Keep Software Updated

Updates patch known security holes. Delaying them leaves a door open.

3

Think Before You Click

Hover over links. Check the real URL. One wrong click can compromise everything.

4

Back Up Your Data

Keep a copy offline. Ransomware becomes powerless when you have backups.

5

Lock Your Devices

A PIN or biometric lock means anyone who grabs your phone can't access your life.

6

Review App Permissions

A calculator doesn't need your location. Revoke unnecessary permissions regularly.

7

Use HTTPS Only

Look for the padlock. Never enter passwords or card details on http:// sites.

8

Talk About It

Share what you know. Your family and friends are also targets.
